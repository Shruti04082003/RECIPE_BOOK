Chocolate chip banana bread

Servings:
8-10
Cooking time:
1 hour
Difficulty:

Description

Rustle up a moreish chocolate chip banana bread – it’s a great way to use up ripe bananas that are lingering in the fruit bowl

Ingredients

  • 150g salted butter
  • 140g unsalted butter, softened, plus extra for the tin
  • 140g golden caster sugar
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 140g self-raising flour
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 3 ripe bananas, peeled and mashed
  • 100g chocolate chips

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 180C/160C fan/gas 4.
  2. Butter a 900g loaf tin and line the base and sides with baking parchment.
  3. Soften the butter if it is not already soft.
  4. Beat the softened butter and sugar together using an electric whisk until the mixture is light and fluffy.
  5. In a separate bowl, combine the egg and vanilla extract. Gradually add this mixture to the butter and sugar, pouring in a little at a time and mixing between each addition.
  6. Sift the flour, add a pinch of salt, and the baking powder into the mixture.
  7. Mash the bananas until smooth and fold them into the mixture.
  8. Fold in all but a handful of the chocolate chips.
  9. Scrape the mixture into the prepared loaf tin and scatter the remaining chocolate chips over the top.
  10. Place the loaf tin in the preheated oven and bake for about 50 minutes or until the loaf is puffed up and cooked through.
  11. Check if the loaf is done by inserting a skewer into the middle; it should come out clean.
  12. Leave the loaf to cool in the tin for 10 minutes, then carefully remove it from the tin and transfer it to a wire rack to cool completely.
  13. Once cool, slice the loaf before serving. Store the sliced loaf in an airtight container, and it will keep for up to five days.
